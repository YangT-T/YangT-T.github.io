This paper proposes a method based on deep learning to approximate Nash equilibria. Firstly, we review the progress in the fields of game theory and deep learning, and summa- rize the computational complexity of Nash equilibrium problem and its traditional solving algorithms. Then, we introduce the theoretical foundations of game theory and deep learn- ing, including normal-form games, mixed strategies, Nash equilibrium, and neural network architecture, which serve as the basis for the neural network-based solving method. Subse- quently, we introduce the Nash approximation loss function and elucidate its effectiveness in solving Nash equilibrium problems. Based on this, we provide a summary and analyze various details involved in the deep learning framework, and design a neural network struc- ture for solving Nash equilibrium. Next, we conduct a series of experiments to analyze the performance of the neural network-based Nash approximator from different perspectives, including solution efficiency, loss function, network structure, transfer learning, and warm- start application. The experimental results demonstrate that our proposed method based on deep learning exhibits good performance and effectiveness in solving Nash equilibrium problems. Although performance declines in some more complex games, convergence can be ensured by enhancing the model’s expressive capability. Finally, we summarize the re- search results of this paper and discuss future directions for interdisciplinary research be- tween deep learning and game theory.
